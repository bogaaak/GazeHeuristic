Gaze Heuristic Simulator

The simulation is based on the fighter vs. bomber example in Hamlin's (2017, in press) biography of the gaze heuristic. In this 'game', the fighter tries to catch the bomber using the gaze heuristic or other strategies (see below).

Playing around under varying conditions (speed, cycle rate) hopefully provides more intuition of when and why the gaze heuristic suceeds or fails. How to play:

  • Use the space bar to play / pause.
  • Reset to starting position using 'r'.
  • Try to fool the gaze heuristic and use 'n' (left) and 'm' (right) to steer the bomber.
  • The 'Tizzy angle' is determined once in the beginning based on the initial position of both airplanes and the bomber's direction. You can recalculate the 'Tizzy angle' at any time or set it manually to any desired angle (in degrees). A 'Tizzy angle' of 0Â° corresponds to the 'AN method' as described in the paper and supposedly used by the German Luftwaffe in WW II.
  • The fighter's cycling rate corresponds to the number of frames the fighter has to wait before it can readjust its direction to keep the 'Tizzy angle' constant. A higher value thus makes the fighter thus more lethargic.
